# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Couples planning weddings (primarily brides-to-be), commercial clients, and music artists scouting a videographer. Predominantly mobile, often in a personal or emotional context — researching vendors, sharing the link with a partner or friend. Also fellow creators and potential collaborators who find their way to the Collab section.

## Product Purpose

Joel Chism's filmmaker portfolio and inquiry driver. It exists to make the right client feel like they already know Joel before they reach out — and to make the wrong client self-select out. Success is an inquiry from someone who says "I just love your vibe."

## Brand Personality

Personal, cinematic, unhurried. Not a corporate studio — a person with a camera and a point of view. Joel describes himself as a "Connector" (Tipping Point sense), youth pastor energy, millennial in a Gen Z body, people person who fades into the background when the moment calls for it. The site should feel like the first five seconds of one of his films.

## Anti-references

- Generic template portfolio sites (Squarespace/Wix filmmaker defaults)
- Overproduced corporate video company sites with stock-footage hero reels
- Hustle-culture "book a consult" SaaS-style service sites
- Sites that lead with pricing or packages before personality

## Design Principles

1. **The filmmaker is the product.** Personality comes through every word and interaction — not in a loud way, but in the way a documentary subject's character emerges quietly from how they move.
2. **Show, don't announce.** The site's own motion and craft should model the quality of Joel's films. A clunky transition is a broken promise.
3. **Earn the scroll.** Every section is a single idea. No filler. The visitor should feel like they're being led, not pushed.
4. **Present when it counts, invisible when it doesn't.** UI should support the work and step aside. Interface chrome should never compete with imagery.
5. **One right person over a thousand wrong ones.** Specificity over reach. It's okay to be exactly Joel — that's the point.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout (already implemented for clap animations and screen quake). Primary audience is mobile-first, so touch target sizing and readable text at default zoom are non-negotiable.
