The Filmmaker · San Diego, CA
Hey, I’m Joel.

I’m Joel — a videographer who points the camera at real people and real moments: weddings, worship nights, brand stories, documentaries. I’m a Christian, a Connector who turns your vision into reality, and a coffee-obsessed geek with youth pastor energy. If that’s your kind of person, let’s make something.

I make videos. Weddings, worship nights, brand stories, the occasional documentary — basically, if it’s real and there are people in it, I want to press record.

I’m a Christian — front page, plain text, not something you have to go digging to find out. If you want to know where I’m coming from, read 1 Corinthians 13.

I got into film in college because I was done with tests and memorization. Film was the opposite: work with people, make something cool. Fell in love on day one and never looked back.

Here’s how I work. I’m a Connector — a term from Malcolm Gladwell’s The Tipping Point for the people who are good at getting things done and connecting the dots (and the right people to each other). I’m not the lone genius dreaming up the big idea; I’m the one who takes your idea and makes it real. So I do my best work alongside people who know what they want, building it together.

Off the clock, I’m a geek — noun: a person with obsessive enthusiasm for a specific subject — and, unlike a nerd, social about it. Current subjects: coffee, custom mechanical keyboards, audio gear, and EDM, just to name a few. I’m learning photography too.

People say I give off youth pastor energy, which tracks: a millennial in a Gen Z body, jokester by default, people person always. I’m an extrovert — though I’ve been told I read like a confident introvert.

If that sounds like your kind of person — let’s make something.
